Duration: 10 sessions of 3 hours each

Learning Objectives

  • Understand and apply key theoretical models in asset pricing
  • Develop ability to create original models
  • Prepare for advanced Empirical Asset Pricing
  • Master the theoretical foundations of modern finance

  • 70% Final Exam
  • 30% Two Homework Assignments

Course Schedule

Day 0: Course Introduction

Presentation of the course and overview of asset pricing theory.

Day 1: Fundamentals of Asset Pricing

Law of one price, stochastic discount factor, Arrow-Debreu framework, and fundamental theorem of asset pricing.

Day 2: Portfolio Choice and Static Models

Mean-variance analysis, HARA utility, CAPM, and Arbitrage Pricing Theory.

Day 3: Dynamic Asset Pricing

Dynamic programming, Merton's model, intertemporal CAPM, and recursive preferences.

Day 4: Fixed-Income and Derivatives

Term structure models, option pricing, and continuous-time asset pricing.

Day 5: Market Frictions

Transaction costs, borrowing constraints, and incomplete markets.

Day 6: Information and Microstructure

Asymmetric information, rational expectations, and market microstructure models.

Day 7: Intertemporal Risk

Long-run risk, rare disasters, and Epstein-Zin preferences.

Day 8: Macro-Finance

Intermediary asset pricing, production-based models, and macro-finance links.

Day 9: Behavioral and Household Finance

Prospect theory, behavioral deviations, and theoretical household finance.
